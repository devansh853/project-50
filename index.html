Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleons

  1. Their feet work like salad tongs.

  2. They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

  3. Skin crystals enable them to change color.

  4. Unlike many lizards, Chameleons can't regrow their tails.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.

  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

  3. Most have red-orange eyes.

  4. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

  5. Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.

Pandas

  1. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown)

  2. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

  3. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons have been present with humans since ages. The pigeons have been around humans for thousands of year.

  2. Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.

  3. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

Bear

  1. Bears are omnivorous mammals divided into eight species and most of them are endangered.

  2. Did you know that to scratch their backs on their favorite trees, they walk for miles.

  3. Canada is home to nearly 60 % of the world’s polar bears.

  4. The Asiatic black bear has the largest ears than other species of bears.

  5. Black bears can run at the speed up to 35mph.

  6. Baloo, from The Jungle Book, is a sloth bear.
